How Kelly works (and why it matters for Polymarket)

The Kelly Criterion tells you the bet size that maximizes long-run logarithmic growth of your bankroll. It's the mathematically optimal answer to "how much should I bet?" given a known edge.

The formula for a binary bet: f* = (b·p − q) / b

where b = decimal odds (net profit per unit staked), p = your probability of winning, q = 1 − p.

Polymarket specifics: if you buy YES at $0.20, you risk $0.20 to win $0.80 per share — so b = 0.80 / 0.20 = 4.0. If you genuinely think YES will resolve at 40%, full Kelly says bet ~25% of your bankroll.

Why fractional Kelly? Full Kelly is path-volatile. A 25% Kelly bet that loses 3 times in a row drops your bankroll 50%+. Most professional bettors use ¼ to ½ Kelly to keep drawdowns survivable. Variance halves; expected growth only drops slightly.

Negative edge? If your estimate doesn't beat the market price after fees, the optimal bet is $0 — Kelly explicitly says don't bet. This calculator will show that result.

Caveats: Kelly assumes you know p. Most retail bettors over-estimate their edge. If you're not 70%+ confident your estimate beats the market's, halve your Kelly fraction. Liquidity on Polymarket also caps real-world stake size — check the order book.
