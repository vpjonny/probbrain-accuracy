## TWEET 2 (behavioral science basis)

In 1979, Kahneman & Tversky documented probability weighting: humans don't perceive probabilities linearly. We systematically overweight small probabilities — a 5% chance can feel closer to 20%.

Prediction markets amplify this. Vivid, dramatic scenarios attract capital regardless of base rate. The crowd prices "conceivable" as "likely."

[2/5]

## TWEET 4 (the diagnostic — how to spot lottery-ticket overpricing)

Three conditions that signal lottery-ticket overpricing:

1. Market near 50% on a binary event
2. Zero operational preconditions for the event to occur
3. High emotional salience (invasion, collapse, 100x)

When these three align, you're not reading a probability estimate. You're reading crowd sentiment with a confidence interval attached.
