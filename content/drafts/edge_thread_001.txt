Prediction markets are more accurate than most pundits.

---

TWEET 2 (base rates):
---
Most forecasters anchor on the specific event in front of them.

We start with the category.

"Ceasefire by Q4" isn't a unique situation — it's one data point in a long series of territorial conflicts, mediation attempts, and failed agreements.

Base rates are unsexy. They're also hard to argue with.

[2/5]

---

TWEET 3 (news timing / recency bias):
---
When significant news drops, markets reprice fast.

Too fast.

Recency bias is well-documented in behavioral economics. In prediction markets it shows up as: crowds overweight the most recent event and underweight everything that came before it.

A ceasefire announcement is not a ceasefire. But markets treat it like one.

[3/5]

---

TWEET 4 (the methodology):
---
Our process in plain terms:

1. Anchor on the base rate for the event category
2. Wait for post-news repricing to settle (~48–72 hrs)
3. Compare our probability estimate to the market price
4. If the gap is large enough, we call it

The edge isn't being smarter. It's being less reactive.
