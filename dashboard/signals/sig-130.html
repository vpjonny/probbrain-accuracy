Evidence
  • United Russia has won every State Duma election since 2003 with commanding majorities (324 of 450 seats in 2021, ~50% of party-list vote).
  • New People won only 13 seats in 2021 (its debut), roughly 5.3% of the party-list vote — an order of magnitude behind United Russia.
  • The Kremlin tightly controls electoral outcomes; United Russia is the explicit party of power backed by Putin and the state apparatus.
  • No credible polling or political dynamic suggests New People is on track to overtake United Russia; if anything, smaller parties' shares are managed downward.
  • Next Duma election is scheduled for September 2026; structural advantages (administrative resource, media control, single-mandate districts) make United Russia's plurality near-certain.
Counter-evidence
  • An unprecedented regime collapse, succession crisis, or war-related shock before September 2026 could in theory upend party dynamics, but no such trigger is currently visible.
