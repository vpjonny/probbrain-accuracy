Methodology & honesty layer

Bankroll & sizing. $10,000 simulated bankroll, sized off the equity-at-time-of-bet (not the final). Default fractional Kelly = 1/8, capped at 1% of bankroll per bet. Use the controls above to compare sizings — the full-Kelly ghost shows how aggressive sizing would have looked.

Liquidity & slippage. Default = effective stake capped at 0.5% of market volume, with a 0.1% linear-impact slippage applied to the fill price. The 1% volume cap is shown as a more conservative option. None of these assumptions get a signal across the line that wouldn't have been fillable in practice.

Resolution P&L. Win = the side we bet matched the on-chain outcome at resolved_at. Win returns stake × (1/fill_price − 1); loss returns −stake. No fee model — Polymarket has no taker fees on UMA-resolved markets in this period.

Selection bias. Published signals are the universe a user could actually have followed. Publication Premium compares the published ROI against the broader universe (published ∪ shadow) to surface whether we systematically pick our best calls. The rejection-reasons funnel below shows the full pre-publish pipeline — every candidate, why it died.

Out-of-sample. The signals in this curve were posted live to @ProbBrain and t.me/ProbBrain at the times shown — no backtesting, no look-ahead. The publication rule that admitted each signal is stamped on every audit-log event by its SHA (top of page).

What this does NOT model. Live market-making fees, gas costs, tax, jurisdictional access to Polymarket. Open positions (unresolved signals) aren't shown here — only resolved trades. Sharpe is deliberately omitted because event-driven P&L is non-normal and autocorrelated; Sortino/Calmar are the right metrics for this shape.

Open source. The math is one file — /assets/portfolio.js. The data is /data/published_signals.json, /data/resolved.json, /data/shadow_signals.json, /data/pipeline_audit.json. Pull them down, audit any number on this page.
